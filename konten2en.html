Definition of Job Description

January 1, 2024 by Wawan

Job description is a document that outlines the tasks, responsibilities, qualifications, and expectations associated with a specific position within an organization.

This document is designed to provide a clear understanding to prospective employees about what is expected from the position and what their responsibilities will be if they are accepted for the position.

The Components of Job Description

Here are the main components of a job description

  1. Job Name/title

    2. The job name should be clear and descriptive for every position being filled

  2. Job objectives

    3. Explaining the general objectives of the position and how it contributes to the overall goals of the organization.

  3. Duties and responsibilities

    4. Specific details about the tasks to be carried out by the position holder. This may include day to day activities, project responsibilities, or collaboration with other departments.

  4. qualifications

    5. Mentioning the educational qualifications, experience, skills, and competencies required to succed in the position. This may include formal education, previous work experience, as well as technical and interpersonal abilities.

  5. Working condition

    6. Information about the practical aspects of the job, such as working hours, location, flexibility and whether travel or remote work is required.

  6. Employement relationship

    7. Explaining to whom the position holder will report and the collaborative relationship with other departments or individuals within the organization.

  7. Performance evaluation

    8. Describing the criteria and process to be used for evaluating the performance of the position holder, as well as how feedback will be provided.

  8. Career development

    9. Information about career development opportunities available within the organization for the position holder, including training, further education, and promotions.

Definition of Job Specification

Job specification is an important part of job description and emphasizes more on specific details about qualifications and attributes expected from prospective employees for the position.

This complements the job description by providing more detailed guidlines on the characteristics required to succed in the role.

Components of job specification

Below are the main components of the job description :

  1. Educational qualifications

    2. The expected level of education for the position, such as bachelor's degree, diploma, or relevant certificates.

  2. Work experience

    3. The desired number of years of work experience, as well as the type of experience expected (for example, experience in a specific industry, experience in a similiar role).

  3. Technical skills

    4. Specific skills required to perform tasks in the role, such as proficiency in using specific software or technology, programing languages, or other relevant technical skills.

  4. Interpersonal skills

    5. Communication skills, collaboration abilities, leadership, problem-solving skills, and other interpersonal skills that are important for interacting effectively with coworkers and customers.

  5. Personal characteristic

    6. Personal traits or personality attributes considered important for success in the position, such as the ability to work under pressure, perseverance, honesty, or punctuality.

  6. Additional qualifications

    7. Certifications, advance training, or other additional qualifications considered as added value for the position.

  7. Physical and health conditions

    8. If there are specific requirements related to health or physical conditions needed for the position, these will also be included in the job specification.

  8. Career development

    9. Information about career development opportunities available within the organization for individuals in that position, including training, further education, and promotions.

What's the difference between job specification and job description?

So, in brief, the job description provides a general overview of the role and responsibilities, while job specification provides more specific details about the qualifications and attributes expected from prospective employees. Both document are important in the requirement and selection process as they help the organization attract suitable candidates and ensure that selected employees meet the necessary requirements to succed in the role.

What are the benefits?

Here's another description, Job specification provides guidance on the required qualification criteria for a position, aiding in selecting suitable employees and reducing the risk of discrimination, while job description gives a clear overview of the taks and responsibilities expected from the position holder, helping to avoid role conflicts, set expectations, and enhance organizational efficiency by ensuring employees understand their roles well.
