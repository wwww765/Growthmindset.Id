Definition of Job Analysis

January 1, 2024 by Wawan

Job analysis is a process involving the collection and examination of key job activities within a position, as well as an assessment of the qualifications required for individuals to perform those activities.

This involves placing the right person according to the abilities, skills, and experience required to perform a particular job. In other words, job analysis helps determine the suitability between individuals and the right job positions based on the qualifications and requirements needed.

The purpose of job analysis

According to Veitzal Rivai, the purpose of job analysis are as follows:

  1. To evaluate the influence of the environment on individual job roles
  2. Refreshing job requirements to support company objectives
  3. Establishment of fair regulations for all parties
  4. Design future human resource needs based on job analysis
  5. Adjusting the number of applicants to available vacancies
  6. Design education and training for new and experienced employees
  7. As a reference for optimizing employee potential development
  8. As a reference for setting realistic performance standards
  9. As a reference for placing employees according to their interests to enhance work motivation
  10. As a reference for placing employees according to their skills to enhance company contributions
  11. As a reference for providing fair compensation for both parties

Methods of gathering job specification information

There are several methods for data collection, including the following :

  • Interview

    An interview is a process of interaction between one or more individuals in a communication process. You can use this method by interviewing employees or you can interview relevant supervisors.

  • Questionnaire

    Questionnaire is a systematically designed set of questions to gather necessary information about various aspects, such as types and details of tasks, responsibilities, required individual characteristics, working conditions, health and safety at work, performance standards, and other aspects.

  • Observation

    Observation is the act of directly observing activities taking place in the field.

  • Participant Observation Notes

    Asking employees about "Activity Logs" related to their activities. Activity logs are daily records made by employees for each activity they perform, also noting the time required for each activity.

You can use one or more of the methods above to gather job specification information.

Then what is the job specification information used for?

Job specification information can be used for purposes as previously described in the objectives, or generally used to create or update job descriptions and job specifications. This is helpful when recruiting employees and in enhancing company performance.
