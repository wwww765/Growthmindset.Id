1% Rules

January 8, 2024 by Wawan

1% in a day might seen insignificant in one's life. However, in reality, if done consistently, it can become a habit and have a significant impact on one's self improvement. In short, consistent small changes can shape our future indentity.

Don't focus too much on the Main Goal

When our minds are fixated on the belief that happiness will be attained upon reaching a goal, then during the journey towards that goal, we'll only feel burdened by the activities that need to be done. Instead, direct our focus on the journey system we created.

One alternative is to break down the main goal into a achievable targets. For example, if we aim to have a six-pack body, we can break down that goal into targets such as exercising at the gym for 10 minutes every day.

When we focus on the goal, we only concentrate on winning the challenge. However, when we focus on the challenge system, we are more oriented towards continuously participating in that system.

If you want to develop good habits, make sure your goals meet these four criteria

  1. Obvious

    2. Create clear and measurable goals. Include details on how to achieve them and what the steps are. By having clear goals, you will find it easier to see the direction to take and the necessary steps to be taken.

  2. Attractive

    3. Make your goals interesting. For example, if you want to complete your tasks, try adding elements that make it engaging, like listening to music while working. This way, you'll be more motivated.

  3. Easy

    4. Make the habit easy to do. Create strategies to make the habit easy to accomplish. For example, if you want to learn to play the guitar, place the guitar in an easily accessible location so you are more inclined to practice it.

  4. Satisfying

    5. Make the habit satisfying. Reward yourself when you successfully achieve the target. This can boost motivation and provide a positive incentive to continue the habit.
